Welcome to In Defense of Defenders (IDOD). This starter post introduces the project and explains how to get involved.

## About this project

IDOD provides practical, low-bandwidth cybersecurity guidance for human rights defenders, journalists, and civil society organizations operating under threat. All content is written for teams with limited resources, limited bandwidth, and high operational stakes.

## What you will find here

- **Threat analysis** — plain-language breakdowns of active campaigns targeting civil society
- **Detection engineering** — portable Sigma, KQL, and YARA rules you can deploy immediately
- **OpSec baselines** — device and communications hygiene for field teams and remote workers
- **Tool reviews** — curated picks from the [Resources directory]({{ '/resources/' | url }}) with honest tradeoffs

## How to contribute

Use the [Contact & Submissions]({{ '/contact/' | url }}) page to suggest topics, request technical help, or share resources you believe should be listed here.
